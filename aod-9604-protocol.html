AOD-9604

Modified HGH fragment for lipolysis

Common Research Focus
Fat Metabolism • Lipolysis • Metabolic Health
Overview

AOD-9604 is a modified fragment of human growth hormone (HGH 177-191) designed to stimulate lipolysis without affecting blood sugar or growth. It is one of the most researched fat-loss peptides with a strong safety profile.

It selectively promotes fat breakdown (lipolysis) while avoiding the insulin and growth effects of full HGH, making it ideal for metabolic and body composition research.

PUBLISHED RESEARCH
Clinical Trials & Studies

AOD-9604, a synthetic lipolytic fragment of human growth hormone (hGH 177-191), has been extensively studied for its fat-reducing properties without the growth-promoting or diabetogenic effects of full-length hGH. Below are key published studies and clinical data.

2013 JEM
Safety and Tolerability of AOD9604 in Humans
Six randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trials demonstrated an excellent safety profile indistinguishable from placebo. No IGF-1 elevation, no impairment of glucose tolerance, no immunogenicity, and no serious adverse events related to AOD-9604.

2000 HORM RES
Metabolic Studies of Synthetic Lipolytic Domain (AOD9604)
In obese Zucker rats, oral AOD-9604 (500 μg/kg daily for 19 days) reduced body weight gain by >50%, increased adipose tissue lipolytic activity, and showed no adverse effects on insulin sensitivity—unlike full-length hGH.
READ THE STUDY
2001 ENDOCRINOLOGY
Effects of hGH and AOD9604 on Lipid Metabolism in Obese Mice
Chronic AOD-9604 treatment in obese mice reduced body weight and fat mass while increasing β3-adrenergic receptor (β3-AR) expression. Effects on energy expenditure and fat oxidation were confirmed in β3-AR knockout models, indicating both β3-AR-dependent and independent mechanisms.
