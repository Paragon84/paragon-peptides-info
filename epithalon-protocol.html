OVERVIEW

Epithalon (also known as Epitalon or Epithalamin) is a synthetic tetrapeptide composed of alanine, glutamic acid, aspartic acid, and glycine. It was developed based on research into pineal gland peptides and is studied for its potential effects on telomerase activation and cellular longevity pathways.

Research has explored Epithalon's potential role in modulating gene expression, supporting cellular repair mechanisms, and influencing circadian rhythm regulation. It is an area of active investigation in aging research and cellular senescence studies.

  • Injection site reactions (mild)
  • Transient fatigue
  • Sleep pattern changes (reported)

Epithalon is generally well-tolerated in research settings with minimal reported adverse effects.
