Melanocortin Agonist Research Grade

Melanotan II

Synthetic melanocortin receptor agonist

Common Research Focus
Skin Pigmentation • Libido • Appetite
Overview

Melanotan II is a synthetic cyclic heptapeptide analog of α-melanocyte-stimulating hormone (α-MSH). It acts as a non-selective melanocortin receptor agonist, primarily targeting MC1R (melanogenesis) and MC4R (appetite and sexual function) receptors.

Originally developed as a potential sunless tanning agent, Melanotan II has been extensively researched for its ability to stimulate melanin production, increase libido, and modulate appetite. It is one of the most studied melanocortin peptides in research settings.

Key Research Benefits
Melanogenesis Stimulation
Promotes natural skin tanning without UV exposure
Libido Enhancement
Strong research interest in sexual arousal and function
Appetite Suppression
May reduce caloric intake via MC4R activation
Skin Protection Research
Potential photoprotective effects through increased melanin
Research Dosing Guidelines
Phase Daily Dose Frequency Notes
Loading Phase 0.25–0.5 mg Daily First 7–14 days
Maintenance Phase 0.5–1.0 mg Every other day or 2–3×/week Once desired pigmentation achieved
Libido Research 0.5–1.0 mg As needed (30–60 min prior) Separate from tanning protocol
Note: Start low to assess tolerance. Many researchers use a gradual ramp-up over the first week. Subcutaneous injection in the abdomen or thigh is standard.

Suggested Research Protocol
1
Days 1–7 (Loading)
0.25 mg daily. Increase by 0.25 mg every 2–3 days as tolerated.
2
Days 8–21 (Build Phase)
0.5–1.0 mg daily until desired skin tone is reached (typically 2–4 weeks).
3
Maintenance
0.5–1.0 mg 2–3 times per week to maintain pigmentation.
Quick Facts
Molecular Weight 1,024.2 Da
Sequence Ac-Nle-c[Asp-His-D-Phe-Arg-Trp-Lys]-NH₂
Half-life ~1–2 hours
Administration Subcutaneous
Storage 2–8°C (reconstituted)
Potential Side Effects
  • Facial flushing (common, usually transient)
  • Nausea or reduced appetite
  • Increased libido (intended effect in some studies)
  • Darkening of freckles/moles
  • Injection site reactions
Flushing and nausea typically decrease with continued use.
