OVERVIEW

NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a critical coenzyme found in every living cell. It plays an essential role in cellular energy production, DNA repair, and gene expression regulation. Research has explored NAD+ supplementation for age-related decline, metabolic health, and cellular resilience.

NAD+ levels naturally decline with age. Research compounds such as NMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide) and NR (nicotinamide riboside) are studied as NAD+ precursors that may support cellular NAD+ levels. Direct NAD+ administration via IV or subcutaneous routes is also an area of active investigation.

POTENTIAL SIDE EFFECTS
  • Flushing, warmth sensation
  • Nausea (dose-dependent)
  • Headache, dizziness
  • Injection site discomfort
  • Fatigue (transient)
IV administration may cause transient flushing; slow infusion rate if needed.
