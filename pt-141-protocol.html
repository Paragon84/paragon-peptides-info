Melanocortin Agonist Research Grade

PT-141

Bremelanotide • MC4R Selective Agonist

Common Research Focus
Sexual Function • Libido • Arousal
Overview

PT-141 (Bremelanotide) is a synthetic cyclic peptide derived from Melanotan II. Unlike its parent compound, PT-141 was specifically engineered to be highly selective for the melanocortin-4 receptor (MC4R), which plays a central role in sexual arousal pathways in both males and females.

It is one of the most researched peptides for sexual dysfunction and libido enhancement. PT-141 works centrally in the brain rather than through vascular mechanisms, making it a unique subject of study in sexual medicine research.

Key Research Benefits
Sexual Arousal
Strong central stimulation of libido and arousal pathways
Works in Both Sexes
Research shows efficacy in male and female models
Non-Vascular Mechanism
Acts on the central nervous system, not blood flow
Rapid Onset
Effects typically observed within 30–60 minutes
Research Dosing Guidelines
Research Use Dose Timing Max per 24h
Standard Protocol 1.0–1.75 mg 30–60 min before activity 1.75 mg
Higher Intensity Studies 1.5–2.0 mg As needed 2.0 mg
Note: PT-141 is typically used on an as-needed basis rather than daily. Subcutaneous injection in the abdomen is the standard route. Do not exceed 2 mg in 24 hours in research settings.

Suggested Research Protocol
1
Single-Dose Studies
Administer 1.0–1.75 mg subcutaneously 30–60 minutes prior to the research observation window.
2
Multiple-Dose Crossover
Separate doses by at least 24–48 hours. Many protocols limit use to 2–3 times per week.
3
Titration (if needed)
Start at 1.0 mg and increase by 0.25–0.5 mg in subsequent sessions based on response.

Potential Side Effects
  • Facial flushing (most common)
  • Nausea (dose-dependent)
  • Headache
  • Injection site reactions
  • Temporary increase in blood pressure
Side effects are generally mild and short-lived. Nausea can be reduced by starting with lower doses.
IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER

PT-141 is for research use only. It is not approved for human therapeutic use outside of specific regulatory contexts. All studies must adhere to strict ethical and safety protocols.
