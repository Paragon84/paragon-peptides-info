Semaglutide

Common Research Doses

OVERVIEW

Semaglutide is a synthetic analogue of human glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) with a prolonged half-life due to albumin binding. It is studied for its effects on glycemic control, appetite regulation, and weight management through GLP-1 receptor activation.

Research has explored semaglutide's potential in type 2 diabetes management and obesity treatment. The compound demonstrates significant effects on glucose-dependent insulin secretion, glucagon suppression, and delayed gastric emptying.
