Mitochondrial Peptide Research Grade

SS-31

Elamipretide (Bendavia) — mitochondria-targeting peptide

Common Research Focus
Mitochondrial Function • Heart/Kidney Health • Oxidative Stress
Overview

SS-31 (Elamipretide, Bendavia) is a synthetic 16-amino acid peptide that selectively targets the inner mitochondrial membrane. It reduces oxidative stress, stabilizes cardiolipin, and improves mitochondrial function and ATP production.

Research focuses on its potential in heart failure, kidney disease, mitochondrial myopathies, and age-related mitochondrial dysfunction.

Key Research Benefits
Mitochondrial Protection
Reduces oxidative stress and stabilizes cardiolipin
Improved Energy Production
Enhances ATP synthesis in mitochondria
Cardioprotective Effects
Research in heart failure and ischemia-reperfusion injury
Renoprotective & Neuroprotective
Studied in kidney disease and mitochondrial disorders

Combination Studies

Potential Side Effects
  • Generally well tolerated in trials
  • Mild injection site reactions
  • Rare: headache, nausea, or transient hypotension (IV)
SS-31 has shown a favorable safety profile in multiple clinical trials for heart and kidney indications.
